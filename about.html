About Me

My background and learning journey

I am an Information Systems student at UMBC with a growing interest in networking, infrastructure, and building systems that are both functional and well designed.

```

Bio

I am Michael Adade, an Information Systems student who enjoys learning how technology, design, and problem solving connect. My strongest interests are in networking, systems, databases, and building technical solutions that support real users and real needs.

As I have grown academically, I have become more interested in how infrastructure works behind the scenes. That includes how networks are segmented, how traffic moves between systems, and how design decisions affect performance, usability, and security.

IS 436 helped me think more clearly about structure before implementation. Instead of jumping straight into coding, I learned to pay attention to requirements, layout, consistency, wireframes, navigation, accessibility, and professional presentation. That shift helped me see that strong systems are not just functional. They are also organized, clear, and built with purpose.

What changed for me

  • Before: I focused mostly on whether a project worked.
  • Now: I also think about structure, usability, and how users move through a system.
  • Before: I saw design as mostly visual.
  • Now: I understand design as clarity, organization, accessibility, and intention.
  • Before: I treated planning as a small step before coding.
  • Now: I see planning, wireframing, and version control as part of building better technical solutions.

Learning Journey

I have continued to grow through hands-on labs, class projects, and technical practice that strengthened both my systems thinking and my confidence.

Design Mindset

I now pay much closer attention to layout, hierarchy, responsiveness, accessibility, and how structure improves the user experience.

Future Goal

My goal is to keep building toward networking and infrastructure roles where I can apply technical skills to secure, reliable, and well organized systems.
```
